CHAP. 81. (55.)ÑWHO INVENTED PRESERVES FOR MUREN®.

C. Hirrus[1] was the first person who formed preserves for the murena; and it was he who lent six thousand of these fishes for the triumphal banquets of C¾sar the Dictator; on which occasion he had them duly weighed, as he declined to receive the value of them in money or any other commodity. His villa, which was of a very humble character in the interior, sold for four millions[2] of sesterces, in consequence of the valuable nature of the stock-ponds there. Next after this, there arose a passion for individual fish. At Bauli,[3] in the territory of Bai¾, the orator Hortensius had some fish-preserves, in which there was a murena to which he became so much attached, as to be supposed to have wept on hearing of its death.[4] It was at the same villa that Antonia,[5] the wife of Drusus, placed earrings upon a murena which she had become fond of; the report of which singular circumstance attracted many visitors to the place.

1. Probably the same person as the C. Hirrius Posthumius, who is mentioned as a voluptuary by Cicero, De Fin. B. ii. c. 22, ¤ 70. Varro speaks of him, as expending the rent of his houses, amounting to twelve millions of sesterces, in bait for his muren¾.

2. This is, probably, the meaning of "quadragies "here, though it has been translated 400,000.

3. See B. iii. c. 9.

4. Porphyry, Tzetzes, and Macrobius relate the same story.

5. See B. vii. c. 18, and B. xxxv. c. 36. Her grandson, Caligula, is supposed to have hastened her death.
