We ought not to omit, while we are speaking of instincts, that among birds the swallow[1] is quite incapable of being taught, and among land animals the mouse; while on the other hand, the elephant does what it is ordered, the lion submits to the yoke, and the sea-calf and many kinds of fishes are capable of being tamed.

1. Albertus Magnus says that swallows can be tamed.
