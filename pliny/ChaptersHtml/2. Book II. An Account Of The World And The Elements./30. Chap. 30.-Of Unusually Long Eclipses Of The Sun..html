CHAP. 30.-OF UNUSUALLY LONG ECLIPSES OF THE SUN.

Eclipses of the sun also take place which are portentous and unusually long, such as occurred when C¾sar the Dictator was slain, and in the war against Antony, the sun remained dim for almost a whole year[1].

1. The dimness or paleness of the sun, which is stated by various writers to have occurred at the time of C¾sar's death, it is unnecessary to remark, was a ph¾nomenon totally different from an eclipse, and depending on a totally different cause.
