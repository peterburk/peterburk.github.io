Fluids are dried up by the heat of the sun; we have therefore regarded it as a masculine star, burning up and absorbing everything[1].

1. Aristotle, Meteor. ii. 1, remarks, that as the sun is continually evaporating the water of the sea, it must eventually be entirely dried up. But we have reason to believe, that all the water which is evaporated by the solar heat, or any other natural process, is again deposited in the form of rain or dew.
