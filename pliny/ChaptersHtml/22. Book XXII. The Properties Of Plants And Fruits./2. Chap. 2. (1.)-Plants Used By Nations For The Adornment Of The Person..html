I remark, in the first place, that there are some foreign nations which, in obedience to long-established usage, employ certain plants for the embellishment of the person. That, among some barbarous peoples, the females[1] stain the face by means of various plants, there can be little doubt, and among the Daci and the Sarmat¾ we find the men even marking[2] their bodies. There is a plant in Gaul, similar to the plantago in appearance, and known there by the name of "glastum:"[3] with it both matrons and girls[4] among the people of Britain are in tile habit of staining the body all over, when taking part in the performance of certain sacred rites; rivalling hereby tile swarthy hue of the ®thiopianls, they go in a state of nature.
