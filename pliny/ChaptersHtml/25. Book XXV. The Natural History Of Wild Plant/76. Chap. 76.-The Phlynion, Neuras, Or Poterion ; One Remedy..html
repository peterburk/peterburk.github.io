CHAP. 76.ÑTHE PHLYNION, NEURAS, OR POTERION ; ONE REMEDY.

Frogs, too, have their venom, the bramble-frog[1] in particular, and I myself have seen the Psylli, in their exhibitions, irritate them by placing them upon flat vessels made red hot,[2] their bite being fatal more instantaneously than the sting even of the asp. One remedy for their poison is the phrynion,[3] taken in wine, which has also the additional names of "neuras"[4] and "poterion:" it bears a small flower, and has numerous fibrous roots, with an agreeable smell.
