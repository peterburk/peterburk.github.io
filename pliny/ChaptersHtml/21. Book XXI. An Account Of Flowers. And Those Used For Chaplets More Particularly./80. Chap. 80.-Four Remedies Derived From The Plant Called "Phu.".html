CHAP. 80.—FOUR REMEDIES DERIVED FROM THE PLANT CALLED "PHU."

The root of the plant which we have mentioned in the same place under the name of "phu,"[1] is given in drink, either bruised or boiled, in cases of hysterical suffocation, and for pains of the chest or sides. It acts as an emmenagogue, and is generally taken in wine.

1. B. xii. c. 26. Either the Valeriana Italica, Fée says, or the Valeriana Dioscoridis of Sibthorpe. The Valeriana phu and the Valeriana officinalis of Linnæus have been suggested by some commentators.
